Timeline of Computers


In 1946, John Presper Eckert created the first computer that we know as ENIAC. The computer was as large as a room and took a lot of space. It was made at the University of Pennslyvania, and was described as the "first general-purpose electronic with operational characteristics,"including memory and arithmetic. However, everything changed when the IBM was launched in August 1981. The IBM was not the first personal computer, but it was the most popular. It cost around $1,565, and included a built-in speaker and the ability to do self-diagnostic checks. It also had Microsoft.
